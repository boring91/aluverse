## Ideal Commercial Client Profile

### Primary Targets

**Strata/Body Corporate**

- Multi-unit residential buildings (10-50 units)
- Window/door replacement programs
- Common area upgrades
- **Typical Value:** $30,000 - $100,000
- **Decision Makers:** Strata manager, owners committee

**Commercial Property Owners**

- Retail strip shops
- Small office buildings
- Industrial units with showrooms
- **Typical Value:** $15,000 - $50,000
- **Decision Makers:** Property owner, commercial agent

**Property Developers**

- Multi-unit residential developments
- Mixed-use buildings
- Staged development projects
- **Typical Value:** $50,000 - $200,000+
- **Decision Makers:** Project manager, developer principal

**Government/Institutional**

- Schools, hospitals, community centers
- Requires tender capability
- Long payment terms but secure
- **Typical Value:** $50,000 - $500,000+
- **Decision Makers:** Procurement officer, facilities manager

## Quoting Strategies for Large Projects

### Pricing Approach

**Cost-Plus Model:**

```
Materials Cost: $X
Labor Cost: $Y (hours × rate)
Overhead: 15%
Margin: 20-28%
Contingency: 5-10%
```

**Pricing Comparison:**
| Project | Size | Value | $/m² |
|---------|------|-------|------|
| P0044 | 216 m² | $85,800 | $397 |
| P0048 | 23.85 m² | $23,540 | $987 |
| P0047 | 6.2 m² | $3,520 | $568 |
| P0038 | 64 m² | $6,160 | $96 |

**Insight:** Smaller projects command higher per-m² rates. P0048 at $987/m² vs P0044 at $397/m² suggests opportunity to quote more aggressively on medium-sized work.

### Payment Terms Structure

| Project Value      | Recommended Structure                          |
| ------------------ | ---------------------------------------------- |
| $20,000 - $50,000  | 30% deposit, 70% on completion                 |
| $50,000 - $100,000 | 30% deposit, 40% at halfway, 30% on completion |
| $100,000+          | 20% deposit, 30/30/20% progress payments       |

### Quote Presentation

Commercial clients expect:

- **Detailed scope of works**
- **Product specifications** (brand, model, ratings)
- **Timeline with milestones**
- **Warranty terms** clearly stated
- **Insurance certificates** attached
- **Professional formatting**

## Financial Planning for Commercial Work

### Cash Flow Management

With $109,340 in active commercial projects, cash flow management is critical:

| Challenge               | Solution                              |
| ----------------------- | ------------------------------------- |
| Large material orders   | Negotiate supplier credit terms       |
| Extended payment cycles | Progress payment structure            |
| Upfront labor costs     | Credit line or reserve fund           |
| Variation management    | Clear scope with change order process |

### Recommended Financial Setup

1. **Business credit line:** $75,000 minimum (up from $50,000)
2. **Supplier accounts:** Net 30 terms with major suppliers
3. **Invoice factoring option:** For cash flow emergencies
4. **Reserve fund:** 3 months operating expenses

## Risk Management

### Commercial Project Risks

| Risk               | Mitigation                                |
| ------------------ | ----------------------------------------- |
| Scope creep        | Detailed written scope, variation process |
| Payment delays     | Progress payments, retention policy       |
| Site access issues | Clear scheduling in contract              |
| Defect claims      | Quality control checklist, photos         |
| Weather delays     | Buffer in timeline, force majeure clause  |

### Contract Essentials

- Clear payment terms and late payment penalties
- Defined scope with exclusions listed
- Variation process requiring written approval
- Warranty terms and exclusions
- Dispute resolution process
- Insurance requirements
